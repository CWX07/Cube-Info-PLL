    G Permutation

    The G permutation is the most complex case for PLL to identify. Therefore, we use unique patterns to identify it. The "headlights" and the "block" are used to help identify this case. The headlights are two same colour of corners facing the same side but the center of the side is different, whereas the "block" is a same coloured center and a corner facing the same side and they are connected.

    G1:

    R2 U R' U R' U' R U' R2 D U' R' U R D'

    G2:

    R' U' R U D' R2 U R' U R U' R U' R2 D

    G3:

    R2 U' R U' R U R' U R2 D' U R U' R' D

    G4:

    R U R' U' D R2 U' R U' R' U R' U R2 D'
